I am an assistant professor at School of Data Science, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen. I work on the broad areas of reinforcement learning, online learning, and learning theory. My recent works have a specific focus on game theoretic settings.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from The Chinese University of Hong Kong in 2020, fortunate to be advised by Siu On Chan and Andrej Bogdanov. Before that, I obtained my B.E. in Information Security from Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2014, under Hai Zhao.

I was visiting Vector Institute as a visiting researcher during Jan-Aug 2024, with Yaoliang Yu, and University of Waterloo as a visiting assistant professor during May-Aug 2023, with Pascal Poupart.

Education

Some topics I am currently investigating

  • Markov decision process and its guarantees in regret bound, convergence, and game-theoretic settings
  • Learning methods for mechanism design and information design among multiple intelligent agents
  • Other topics in learning theory and game theory that I find interesting

Perspective students

I am recruiting PhD students for Fall 2025 enrollment. The degree will be issued by CUHK for PhD in Computer Science or PhD in Data Science. See the program information.

For candidates, I mostly care about their math background and their motivation on working on new and explorative problems. We emphasize novelty in research, including new problem settings, new key lemmas, and applications of advanced tools in mathematics. I also host postdocs and visitors, with similar qualifications, if they are willing to spend at least a year with us.
